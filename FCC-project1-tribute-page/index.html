A short history of PowerShell

  • PowerShell started out as and idea by Jeffrey Snover to integrate the previous CLI with scripting, making it capable of automating a wide range of administrative tasks.
  • In August 2002, the Monad Manifesto was published, introducing this idea.
  • In June 2005, Microsoft published the first public beta release.
  • Microsoft announced in April 2006 Monad was renamed Windows PowerShell.
  • Powershell v1 was released shortly after.
  • Powershell v2 was released in August 2009.
  • Microsoft announced it made Powershell open-source and cross-platform in August 2016.
